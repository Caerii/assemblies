# References

Use this page for the literature behind the package and research program.

The package implements and experiments with ideas from these papers; it should
not be cited as the proof of the theory itself.

## Assembly Calculus

- Papadimitriou, C. H., Vempala, S. S., Mitropolsky, D., Collins, M., and
  Maass, W. (2020). *Brain Computation by Assemblies of Neurons*. PNAS,
  117(25), 14464-14472. <https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2001893117>

## Sequences And Computation

- Dabagia, M., Papadimitriou, C. H., and Vempala, S. S. (2023/2024).
  *Computation with Sequences of Assemblies in a Model of the Brain*. ALT
  2024 / arXiv:2306.03812. <https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.03812>

## Language Organ And NEMO

- Mitropolsky, D., and Papadimitriou, C. H. (2023). *The Architecture of a
  Biologically Plausible Language Organ*. arXiv:2306.15364.
  <https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.15364>
- Mitropolsky, D., and Papadimitriou, C. H. (2025). *Simulated Language
  Acquisition in a Biologically Realistic Model of the Brain*. arXiv:2507.11788.
  <https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2507.11788>

## Background

- Hebb, D. O. (1949). *The Organization of Behavior: A Neuropsychological
  Theory*. Wiley.
- Hopfield, J. J. (1982). Neural networks and physical systems with emergent
  collective computational abilities. PNAS, 79(8), 2554-2558.
- Olshausen, B. A., and Field, D. J. (1997). Sparse coding with an
  overcomplete basis set: a strategy employed by V1? *Vision Research*,
  37(23), 3311-3325.
