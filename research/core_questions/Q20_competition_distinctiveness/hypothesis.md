# Q20 Hypothesis: Competition and Distinctiveness

## Central Question

When several stimuli share the same cortical area, can the system still form
distinct and recoverable assemblies, and what role does shared competition play
in that outcome?

## Working Hypothesis

In the tested same-brain stim-only regime, shared competition is sufficient to
produce near-chance overlap between assemblies while preserving stimulus-specific
reactivation. The stronger causal claim that competition alone explains the full
effect still needs a cleaner mechanism-level decomposition.

## Scope

- This question is about representational distinctiveness in shared areas.
- It does not yet prove a full causal decomposition of all mechanism pieces.
- It is distinct from autonomous attractor maintenance questions.

## Predictions

1. Same-brain assemblies should remain near chance overlap.
2. Reactivating a stimulus should recover its trained assembly.
3. Larger networks should reduce the small excess overlap caused by hub neurons.
