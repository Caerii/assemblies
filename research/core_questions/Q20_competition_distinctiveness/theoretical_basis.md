# Theoretical Basis

The Assembly Calculus picture already assumes sparse competition inside a
target area. This question asks whether that shared competition leads to
distinct representations rather than collapse.

In the repo, the relevant ingredients are:

- winner-take-all competition in a shared target area
- repeated stimulus-to-area Hebbian strengthening
- support growth and neuron reuse constraints in explicit areas

Important qualification:

- The repo has strong same-brain distinctiveness results.
- The follow-up mechanism experiment does not yet isolate a single simple cause
  such as "higher beta always helps" or "interleaving always helps."
- So the scientific status is "same-brain competition matters in the tested
  system," not "the full mechanism is solved."
