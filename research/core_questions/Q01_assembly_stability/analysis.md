# Analysis

The current evidence supports a narrower claim than the old tracker language
did.

What we can say:

- recurrent training produces autonomous persistence well above chance
- self-connectome training is causally important in the tested setup
- the strength of stability depends strongly on regime

What we should not say:

- that all sparse assemblies always converge rapidly
- that the repo has already solved multi-attractor stability
- that the single-assembly "global basin" result transfers unchanged to
  competitive settings

The most important scientific implication is that "assembly stability" is not a
single number in this repo. It depends on whether we are asking about:

- a single heavily trained attractor
- a lighter projection regime
- continued recurrence vs one-step recovery
- isolated vs competing assemblies
